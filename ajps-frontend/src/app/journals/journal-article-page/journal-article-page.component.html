Abstract

Rural communities in Bangladesh rely heavily on medicinal plants for treating common health issues, particularly in areas with limited access to modern medical facilities and rich plant biodiversity. This ethnobotanical study, conducted in Bandhakhola village, Gazipur, identifies key medicinal plants used by the local population. Data were analyzed using two quantitative methods: Informant Consensus Factor (Fic) and Fidelity Level (Fl). The Fic analysis revealed respiratory diseases (Fic: 0.82), first aid (Fic: 0.76), and gastrointestinal disorders (Fic: 0.58) as the most significant disease categories. Using Fl, the most utilized species for these categories were identified as Justicia adhatoda (respiratory), Aerva sanguinolenta (first aid), and Litsea glutinosa (gastrointestinal). The study further highlights five critical medicinal plants in the region: Justicia adhatoda, Azadirachta indica, Centella asiatica, Ocimum sanctum, and Cynodon dactylon. These findings provide a foundation for future ethnopharmacological research in the area.
